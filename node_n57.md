## **Verified Context from Research**

### **Technical Specifications:**
- **Thickness**: 1.5mm body (3.2mm with protrusions) - thin as paper yet generates strong forces
- **Structure**: 24 pneumatic chambers + 6-channel pneumatic circuits in multilayer configuration
- **Biomimetic Design**: Mimics myosin motor proteins that power muscle contractions through repeated small movements
- **Performance**: Can lift objects 77x its own weight (227g load, 2.94g actuator weight)
- **Multi-directional**: 6 different movement directions (up/down, left/right, clockwise/counterclockwise rotation)

### **Revolutionary Fabrication Method:**
- **Multilayer lithography**: Enables complex 3D pneumatic networks impossible with traditional casting
- **Selective plasma bonding**: Creates precise pneumatic chambers between PDMS layers
- **Scalable process**: Can be manufactured in large areas or connected in parallel arrays
- **Operating pressure**: 30-60 kPa (safe for human interaction, works underwater)

### **Key Research Team:**
- **Dr. Hyung Gon Shin**: Samsung Electronics' Future Robotics Division (formerly POSTECH PhD)
- **Professor Keehoon Kim**: POSTECH Department of Mechanical Engineering
- **Professor Wan Kyun Chung**: POSTECH Department of Mechanical Engineering
- **Publication**: Nature Communications (impact factor 17.7), peer-reviewed breakthrough

### **Demonstrated Applications:**
- **Pipe inspection**: Navigates 39mm diameter pipes with 33mm camera (3mm clearance)
- **In-hand manipulation**: Enables simple 2-finger grippers to perform complex object repositioning
- **Underwater operation**: Functions safely in aquatic environments
- **Heavy lifting**: Moves objects weighing 77x actuator's own weight
- **Flexible attachment**: Works when bent around 8.8mm diameter cylindrical objects
